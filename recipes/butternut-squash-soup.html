Butternut Squash Soup

Description

My favorite fall tradition is making butternut squash soup on Sunday afternoons. On a brisk day, there's nothing more soothing than chopping and simmering vegetables over a warm stove. As you cook, the kitchen fills with the cozy scent of autumn spices and butternut squash, and when you're finished, steaming bowls of butternut squash soup (that will last all week!) reward you for your work.

Ingredients

  • Fresh butternut squash (3 lbs)
  • 4 large carrots
  • 1 large white onion
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • Chicken stock (4 cups)
  • Spoonful of maple syrup
  • Spices: (measured with the heart)
    • Salt, pepper, cayenne, smoked paprika, nutmeg, cinnamon, turmeric (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F
  2. Cut butternut squash in half vertically, remove seeds. Lay face-down on a baking sheets along with carrots. Coat everything in olive oil and grind salt and pepper. Poke holes in tops of squash. Put baking sheet in oven until the squash skin is brown.
  3. Saute chopped onion and garlic in a large pot until caramelized. Add squash and carrots. Add spices and fry until fragrant.
  4. Add chicken stock and maple syrup. Let it simmer for 15 minutes or so to let flavors blend. Salt to taste.
  5. Immersion blend the mixture until smooth.
